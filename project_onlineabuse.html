Francielle Vargas

University of São Paulo & Federal University of Minas Gerais.

I am a software engineer and linguist with MSc and Ph.D. (candidate) in Natural Language Processing. My current mentors are Prof. Fabrício Benevenuto of the Computer Science Department at Federal University of Minas Gerais (DCC-UFMG), and Prof. Thiago A. S. Pardo of the Institute of Mathematics and Computer Science at University of São Paulo (ICMC-USP). I am also a member of Social Computing Lab (LOCUS) and Interinstitutional Center for Computational Linguistics (NILC).

My primary research is in the area of Natural Language Processing (NLP). My research lies mainly on computational methods that address theories underlying discourse and pragmatics analysis for a wide range of real-world applications, as well as social media processing. Furthermore, I have experience working on entity and relation extraction, language generation, summarization, opinon mining-sentiment analysis, computational argumentation, and internalization projects.

I obtained my M.Sc. in Computer Science and Computational Mathematics from University of São Paulo (USP) in 2017. As part of my Master's Thesis , I developed a new method based on semantic relations and linguistic rules to automatically detect fine-grained opinions from online consumer reviews for aspect-based sentiment analysis and summarization. Previously, I obtained B.A. in Linguistics from Federal University of Minas Gerais (UFMG) in 2014, and B.S. in Information Systems from Pontifical Catholic University of Minas Gerais (PUCMG) in 2009.
