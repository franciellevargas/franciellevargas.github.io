Francielle Vargas

Ph.D. Candidate in Computer Science - Natural Language Processing

I am a computer and language scientist with MSc and Ph.D. (candidate) in Natural Language Processing. My current mentors are Prof. Fabrício Benevenuto of the Computer Science Department at Federal University of Minas Gerais (DCC-UFMG), and Prof. Thiago A. S. Pardo of the Institute of Mathematics and Computer Science at University of São Paulo (ICMC-USP). I am also a research member of Social Computing Lab (LOCUS) and Interinstitutional Center for Computational Linguistics (NILC).

My primary research is in the area of Natural Language Processing (NLP). My research lies mainly in the investigation and development of safe and trusty real-world systems, as well as computational techniques that address theories underlying discourse and pragmatics analysis for language understanding and language generation. Furthermore, I have experience working on entity and relation extraction, summarization, opinon mining-sentiment analysis, computational argumentation, social media processing and internalization projects.

I obtained my M.Sc. in Computer Science and Computational Mathematics from University of São Paulo (USP) in 2017. As part of my Master's Thesis , I developed a new method based on semantic relations and linguistic rules to automatically detect fine-grained opinions from online consumer reviews for aspect-based sentiment analysis and summarization. Previously, I obtained B.A. in Linguistics from Federal University of Minas Gerais (UFMG) in 2014, and B.S. in Information Systems from Pontifical Catholic University of Minas Gerais (PUCMG) in 2009.
