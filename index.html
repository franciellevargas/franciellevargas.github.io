Francielle Vargas

University of São Paulo, São Carlos, Brazil

I am a software engineer and linguist passionate about natural language processing! Currently, I am a Research Scientist Intern at Sinch and a Ph.D. Candidate in Computer Science and Computational Mathematics at University of São Paulo, Brazil. My Advisor is Prof. Thiago A. S. Pardo at Institute of Mathematics and Computer Sciences - University of São Paulo and my Co-Advisor is Prof. Fabrício Benevenuto at Computer Science Departament - Federal University of Minas Gerais. Futhermore, I am a member of Social Computing Lab (LOCUS) and Interinstitutional Center for Computational Linguistics (NILC)<\a>.

My main research concentrates on Natural Language Processing and Computational Linguistics area. I have been working on the development of real-world systems in order to minimize online harms. My current research projects is related to multilingual abusive language and fake news detection on social media. In addiation, I also am especially interesed on discourse and pragmatics aware explainable machine learning models for natural language processing.

I obtained my M.Sc. in Computer Science and Computational Mathematics at the University of São Paulo (USP) under the supervision of Prof. Thiago A. S. Pardo. As part of my Master’s thesis, I developed a algorithm based on semantic relations and linguistic rules to detect fine-grained opionion from online consumers reviews.
