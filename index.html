Francielle Vargas

Ph.D. Candidate in Computer Science - Natural Language Processing
E-Mail | Google Scholar | ORCID | GitHub | Linkedin | Lattes | Curriculum Vitae |

I am a computer and language scientist with an M.Sc. and Ph.D. (final year) in Natural Language Processing. During my Ph.D, I was a visiting scientist at the University of Southern California (2024). I received my M.Sc. in Computer Science and Computational Mathematics from the University of São Paulo (2017). Previously, I obtained a B.S. in Information Systems and a B.A. in Linguistics. I am interested in Natural Language Processing, Machine Learning and Computational Social Science. My research focus on improvement the explainability, robustness, and fairness of language models mostly focus on misinformation and hate speech applications. I rely on machine learning techniques including neural networks to design and guide the development of safer, trustworthy and socially responsible human language technologies.

Research Interests
  • fact-checking, fake news and media bias detection, misinformation, factuality
  • hate speech and offensive language detection, radicalism, bias mitigation, fairness
  • opinion and argument mining, emotion, sentiment and stylistic analysis, subjectivity
  • discourse level-language understanding, low-resource languages, indigenous languages
