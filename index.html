Francielle Vargas

University of São Paulo, São Carlos, Brazil.

I am a software engineer and linguist with MSc and Ph.D. (candidate) in natural language processing. My Ph.D. advisors are Prof. Fabrício Benevenuto of the Computer Science Departament at Federal University of Minas Gerais (DCC-UFMG), and Prof. Thiago A. S. Pardo of the Institute of Mathematics and Computer Science at University of São Paulo (ICMC-USP). I am also a member of Social Computing Lab (LOCUS) and Interinstitutional Center for Computational Linguistics (NILC).

My primary research is in the area of Natural Language Processing (NLP). My research lies mainly on methods and techniques that address theories underlying discourse and pragmatic analysis, as well as the development of real-world applications for detecting deception and mitigating online abuse and harms. Furthermore, I have experience working on relation extraction, summarization, sentiment analysis-opinion mining, computational argumentation, and internalization projects.

I obtained my M.Sc. in Computer Science and Computational Mathematics at University of São Paulo (USP) in 2017. As part of my master's thesis , I developed a method based on semantic relations and linguistic rules to automatically detect fine-grained opinions from online consumer reviews for aspect-based sentiment analysis and summarization. Previously, I obtained B.A. in Linguistics from Federal University of Minas Gerais (UFMG) in 2014, and B.S. in Information Systems from Pontifical Catholic University of Minas Gerais (PUCMG) in 2009.
