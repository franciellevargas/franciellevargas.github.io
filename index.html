Francielle Vargas

São Carlos, Brazil

I am a software engineer and linguist. Currently, I am a research scientist intern at Sinch and Ph.D. candidate in natural language processing at Institute of Mathematics and Computer Science from University of São Paulo (ICMC-USP). My advisor is Prof. Thiago A. S. Pardo and my co-advisor Prof. Fabrício Benevenuto at Computer Science Departament from Federal University of Minas Gerais (DCC-UFMG). Furthermore, I am a member of Social Computing Lab (LOCUS) and Interinstitutional Center for Computational Linguistics (NILC).

My primary research is in the area of natural language processing (NLP). I have been working on the development of real-world systems towards mitigating online abuse and harms. I also have experience working on relation extraction, summarization, sentiment analysis-opinon mining, discourse parsing, and internalization projects.

I have obtained my M.Sc. in Computer Science and Computational Mathematics at University of São Paulo (USP) in 2017 under the supervision of Prof. Thiago A. S. Pardo. As part of my master's thesis , I developed a method based on semantic relations and linguistic rules to automatically detect fine-grained opinion from online consumers reviews for aspect-based sentiment analysis and summarization. Previously, I graduated in Linguistics at Department of Linguistics from Federal University of Minas Gerais (UFMG) in 2014, and in Information Systems at Department of Sofware Enginenering and Information Systems from Pontifical Catholic University of Minas Gerais (PUCMG) in 2009.
