Francielle Vargas

Ph.D. Candidate in Computer Science - Natural Language Processing

I am a computer and language scientist with MSc and Ph.D. (candidate) in Natural Language Processing. My current mentors are Prof. Fabrício Benevenuto of the Computer Science Department at Federal University of Minas Gerais (DCC-UFMG), and Prof. Thiago A. S. Pardo of the Institute of Mathematics and Computer Science at University of São Paulo (ICMC-USP). I am also a research member of Social Computing Lab (LOCUS) and Interinstitutional Center for Computational Linguistics (NILC).

My primary research is in the area of Natural Language Processing (NLP). My research lies mainly in the investigation and development of fair, trust, and safe large-scale real systems, as well as computational techniques for discourse and pragmatic level-language understanding and generation. I rely on machine learning techniques, including neural networks, to model and guide natural language system development. Furthermore, I have experience working on entity and relation extraction, aspect-based opinion mining-sentiment analysis, text summarization, discourse and dependency parsing, computational argumentation, social media processing, and internalization projects. Some of the topics that I am currently researching include:

  • fact-checking, fake news and media bias detection, misinformation
  • hate speech and abusive language detection, bias mitigation, fairness
  • opinion mining-sentiment analysis, stylistic analysis, argument mining
  • discourse and pragmatic aware computational methods and applications

    • I obtained my M.Sc. in Computer Science and Computational Mathematics from University of São Paulo (USP) in 2017. As part of my Master's Thesis , I developed a new method based on semantic relations and linguistic rules to automatically detect fine-grained opinions from user-generated content (UGC) for aspect-based sentiment analysis and summarization. Previously, I obtained B.A. in Linguistics from Federal University of Minas Gerais (UFMG) in 2014, and B.S. in Information Systems from Pontifical Catholic University of Minas Gerais (PUCMG) in 2009.
